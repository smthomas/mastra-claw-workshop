Article 1

Our newsroom AI policy

Ars Technica · Updated Apr 22, 2026
  • Ars positions AI as a tool to support journalism, not replace human writers, editors, or judgment.
  • Published work remains human-authored and human-reviewed.
  • AI can assist with tasks like editing, research support, and brainstorming, but outputs are not treated as authoritative.
  • Any AI-assisted material must be verified by staff before publication.
  • AI-generated visuals are not presented as authentic documentary evidence, and synthetic media must be clearly labeled.
  • Responsibility stays with the human using the tool: staff are accountable for accuracy and transparency.

Ping-pong robot beats top-level human players

Reuters · AI + robotics
  • The story highlights a major milestone in embodied AI: a robot system outperforming high-level human table tennis players.
  • Ping-pong is a demanding benchmark because it combines perception, prediction, rapid control, and adaptation in real time.
  • The result suggests continued progress in robotics where AI is tightly coupled with physical interaction and motor precision.
  • Success in this domain has implications beyond sports, including factory automation, dynamic manipulation, and responsive human-robot collaboration.
  • The broader takeaway is that AI progress is not just about language models; fast, physical decision-making is advancing too.

Article 3

Introducing Parallel Agents in Zed

Zed blog · Developer tools
  • Zed now supports running multiple agents in parallel in the same workspace through a new Threads Sidebar.
  • Agents can be scoped to specific folders or repos, making parallel task delegation more manageable.
  • The product emphasizes speed and fluidity, consistent with Zed’s high-performance, 120 fps design philosophy.
  • Zed frames this as “agentic engineering”: humans directing multiple AI collaborators while retaining craftsmanship and oversight.
  • The update also changes the default layout, placing the Threads Sidebar and Agent Panel on the left.
  • Parallel Agents is available in the latest Zed release and aligns with open, composable developer workflows.
